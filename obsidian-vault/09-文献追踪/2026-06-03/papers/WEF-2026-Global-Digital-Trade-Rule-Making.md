## 摘要

The State of Global Digital Trade Rule-Making in 2026 examines the shifting landscape of digital trade governance, focusing on regional approaches emerging from ASEAN and Africa. The report argues that as multilateral negotiations (e.g., WTO Joint Statement Initiative on E-commerce) face continued deadlock, regional frameworks are becoming the primary laboratories for digital trade rule design. It provides a comparative analysis of the ASEAN Digital Economy Framework Agreement (DEFA) and the AfCFTA Digital Trade Protocol, identifying common challenges and divergent strategies in areas including cross-border data flows, digital product non-discrimination, e-commerce moratorium, and digital trade facilitation. The report finds that while both regions are pioneering South-led digital trade governance, the issue of digital rules of origin remains largely unaddressed — constituting a significant regulatory gap.

## 核心发现

1. ASEAN DEFA作为全球首个区域性数字经济治理协定，采取"有条件开放"的数据跨境流动模式
2. AfCFTA数字贸易议定书采用"非对称承诺"机制应对成员国数字鸿沟
3. 数字产品的原产地认定尚未成为核心谈判议题 —— 构成"规则空白"
4. 建议推动多边e-commerce moratorium永久化，建立区域间互操作性机制
