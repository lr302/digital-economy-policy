## 摘要

This OECD Trade Policy Paper develops the first comprehensive taxonomy of domestic approaches to cross-border data flow regulation and local storage requirements, covering 136 countries and regions through systematic coding of 194 policy documents. The paper identifies four regulatory models: (1) Open Transfers, (2) Conditional Openness, (3) Restricted, and (4) Developing/Undefined. It then surveys international trade instruments — including CPTPP, USMCA, DEPA, and the WTO e-commerce negotiations — that address cross-border data flows, analyzing the extent to which these instruments bridge or fail to bridge the gaps between different domestic regulatory models. The paper's typology has significant implications for digital trade origin rules, as cross-border data flow restrictions directly affect the feasibility of determining the "economic nationality" of digital products and services. The paper also highlights the emerging frontier of AI training data flows and their implications for AI model "origin" determination.

## 核心发现

1. 建立首个涵盖136个经济体的跨境数据流动监管四类型学
2. 四类模式：开放流动型 / 条件开放型 / 限制型 / 发展中/未定义型
3. 现有RTAs的"原则自由+合理例外"框架无法弥合类型间根本分歧
4. AI训练数据的跨境流动将成为下一轮监管争论焦点
