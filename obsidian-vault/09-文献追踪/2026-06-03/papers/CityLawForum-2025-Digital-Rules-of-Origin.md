## 摘要

This conceptual paper, published on the City Law Forum blog, makes the case that "Digital Rules of Origin" should be recognized as a distinct category within international trade law. It argues that the WTO Agreement on Rules of Origin, while providing standardized procedures for calculating the origin of goods, is entirely silent on digital trade scenarios — creating a fundamental legal gap. The paper analyzes the unique challenges of determining origin for digital products and services, including the "multi-location production" dilemma (code in country A, servers in B, data from C users, AI training in D, payments in E), the inapplicability of traditional value-added percentage standards to data-driven products, and the inadequacy of HS code classification for intangible goods. The paper predicts that digital rules of origin will transition from a neglected issue to a core negotiating topic in next-generation regional digital trade agreements.

## 核心发现

1. 首次明确提出"Digital Rules of Origin"作为独立的国际贸易法分析范畴
2. 系统揭示数字产品的"多地点生产困境"——传统ROO标准难以适用
3. 指出增值百分比标准面临数据价值评估难题，HS编码不适用于无形产品
4. 预测ASEAN DEFA等新一代区域协定将纳入数字原产地规则条款
